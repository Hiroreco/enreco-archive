## Overview

**Chapter:** 3
**Duration:** Day 1 to 6

Once a righteous knight who saw Nerissa as little more than an acquaintance—and more importantly, a witch—Elizabeth found her beliefs turned upside down after seemingly falling under Nerissa's spell. As the days passed, she grew increasingly enthralled by the blue witch, slowly losing pieces of herself until she became little more than a puppet for Nerissa to toy with.

## Details

### How it started

It was never made entirely clear when or how Nerissa first cast her spell upon Elizabeth. What was apparent, however, was that from the very first day of Chapter 3, the scarlet knight was behaving differently.

Where Elizabeth would normally challenge or question Nerissa's decisions, she instead trusted her almost unconditionally. She justified this behavior as merely fulfilling a promise to serve as Nerissa's knight, yet her actions suggested something deeper. Time and time again, Elizabeth showed an unusual willingness to follow Nerissa's suggestions, no matter how questionable they might have been.

At first, it was subtle enough to be dismissed.

But it only grew worse.

### How it went

With each passing day, Elizabeth seemed to fall further under Nerissa's influence.

The knight herself appeared completely unaware of how severe the situation had become. She agreed with nearly everything Nerissa said, followed almost every request she was given, and even participated in actions that her former self would have strongly opposed. Though some part of her seemed to recognize that certain choices were wrong—even criminal, at times—she continually pushed those doubts aside in favor of obeying the witch.

In doing so, Elizabeth gradually abandoned the ideals and principles she had spent her entire journey upholding.

The change was not only visible in her actions but in her appearance as well.

Day by day, she grew paler. Sicker. Bluer.

What had initially seemed like a minor change slowly became impossible to ignore. Her skin tone shifted, her eyes changed color, and her overall demeanor became increasingly unsettling. Friends and allies voiced their concerns, yet Elizabeth herself chose to ignore the signs, refusing to acknowledge what was happening to her.

Nerissa, meanwhile, was fully aware of the effect she had on the knight.

The witch knew Elizabeth was wrapped around her finger, and she appeared to enjoy every second of it. Whether through questionable decisions, morally dubious schemes, or outright manipulation, Nerissa repeatedly pushed Elizabeth into situations that conflicted with everything the knight stood for. She wanted Elizabeth by her side, loyal to her and her alone, and she had little concern for the consequences.

At some point, it stopped resembling a romance altogether.

What remained was a story of manipulation, obsession, and a witch who held an alarming amount of power over someone who could no longer bring herself to resist.

### How it ended

The situation became even more complicated on Day 4, when Nerissa regained memories of her past self.

The memories painted a picture that seemed completely at odds with the woman she had become. The Nerissa of old had been kind-hearted, heroic, and self-sacrificing—a person willing to give everything to save others. The Nerissa standing in the present was a witch driven by selfish desires and questionable intentions.

Faced with these conflicting versions of herself, Nerissa found herself genuinely confused.

Who was she supposed to be? Which version was the real one?

For a brief moment, she even considered lifting the spell from Elizabeth. Yet hesitation won out, and in the end, she did nothing.

Meanwhile, Elizabeth's condition continued to deteriorate.

By the final days of the journey, she had become completely blue, both literally and figuratively. Her behavior was erratic, her words often shocked those around her, and many struggled to recognize the knight they once knew. Yet through it all, her devotion to Nerissa remained unwavering.

And so the story came to an end without a true resolution.

Would Elizabeth eventually break free from the witch's influence?

Would Nerissa finally choose to let her go?

Or would the knight remain trapped within the witch's grasp?

Only time would tell.

### Notes

- Elizabeth was almost exclusively referred to as "Lady Bloodflame" throughout Chapter 3. For the sake of consistency with previous stories and entries, this recap refers to her as "Elizabeth".
