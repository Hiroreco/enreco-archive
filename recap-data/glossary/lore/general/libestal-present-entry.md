## Overview

Present Libestal, as the name suggests, was the current-day Libestal—the same kingdom seen in [Chapter 2](#entry:libestal-ancient-entry), but roughly one thousand years later. It was here that the heroes embarked on their journey during Chapter 3.

## Details

### Preface

After failing to stop the Outsider from coming into existence, Fia was left at a loss for what to do. However, she eventually found a clue hidden within the book of poetry he had given her: he could be found "back where it all began."

With the heroes' help, she navigated the Library back to present-day Libestal, which had somehow escaped its fated destruction and now drifted through the far reaches of the multiverse.

### Setting

Upon arriving in Libestal, the heroes discovered that much had changed. The kingdom was plagued by dimensional rifts known as anomalies, as well as dangerous otherworldly visitors called _Strangers._

Mistaken for Strangers themselves, the heroes were confronted by the kingdom's guards before being rescued by a local rebel named Nix, who possessed unusual abilities. Nix then offered them a choice: join her group, the _Rebellion,_ or remain with Fia and the _Shining Stars._

As they settled into the kingdom, they learned that a mysterious figure resembling the Outsider had been sighted in Libestal before vanishing into one of the anomalies.

Just like in Chapter 2, there were also four main [occupations](#entry:jobs-entry) available for the heroes to pursue.

### Appearance

Present-day Libestal closely resembled the kingdom from Chapter 1. Its overall layout, atmosphere, and architecture shared many similarities with Libestal Ficta, including the colosseum and the quartz-built castle. However, some familiar landmarks were grander, larger, and more refined than their earlier counterparts.

Much like in Chapter 2, a temple-like structure stood at the center of the kingdom. This building served as the entrance to the [Library](#entry:library-entry), where [Fia](#entry:iphania-entry) resided.

**Kingdom Houses**

One notable difference from previous versions of the kingdom was the introduction of pre-built houses. Heroes could claim their own homes within Libestal, decorate them, open shops, or share them with others, allowing even those with little talent for construction to have a place to call home.

**Terminus**

Within the kingdom existed a rift in space that served as a gateway to the Prison at the End of Infinity: [Terminus](#entry:terminus-entry). Through this passage, heroes could travel back and forth to the realm where [Faust](#entry:outsider-entry) resided.

### Quests

Quests functioned much as they had before. Side quests provided income and power-ups, while main quests advanced the story and helped restore lost memories.

The final quest and ultimate objective took place within a realm known as [Nullspace](#entry:nullspace-entry). Before reaching it, however, the heroes first had to undergo a trial known as the [Rite of Purification](#entry:rite-of-purification-entry).

### Ending

After overcoming the trials of Nullspace, the heroes discovered that Faust had been waiting for them all along.

There, he revealed the Egg of Creation—an artifact capable of creating, or recreating, the countless worlds scattered across the myriad realms.

Faust then unveiled the truth: **he** was the Outsider. From the very beginning, he had guided the heroes toward this moment so that he could harness their power of infinite potential—the power of Revelation—and use it to awaken the Egg.

He asked whether they would lend him their strength and help him remake all worlds anew. The heroes each gave their answers, but before the Outsider could respond, the conversation was interrupted by the Curse once more.

As the Outsider and the Curse prepared to clash, Fia urgently warned the heroes. At that moment, the Looking-Glass Shard activated once again, whisking them away to an unknown destination.
