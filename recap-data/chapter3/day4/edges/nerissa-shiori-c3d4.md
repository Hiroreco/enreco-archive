Bonded by the advent of the past, tied to the coven in the present, and now a future of conspiracy brings the two closer as the fourth day dawns on Libestal.

Sister Shiori and Nerissa Juliet met in the morning only to find a trail of blood. It weaved between each house, leaving an eerie sheep, that seemed to watch their every movement, and a chest of [cryptic messages](https://youtu.be/60_Pduc0ODY?t=625).

The Illumination flags posted on the walls of each house only pushed the dark clouds over their heads.

Roa was no doubt responsible for the Illumination flags; the widow's insanity grew by the day ever since her wife had passed. Though for the blood and sheep? That remained doubtful.

The mystery of The Flock and the poetry remained to be seen.

The true conspiracy was what lurked beneath the Church of Inkwell.

Beckoned by Sister Shiori, Nerissa Juliet helped, digging the tunnel and supplying her with the materials for this mysterious project throughout the day.

Even defending Sister Shiori when the conspiracy was almost revealed to the unworthy, using [her horn to silence them](https://youtu.be/60_Pduc0ODY?t=13571).

Eventually, Sister Shiori would further reveal her grand plan:

[A Gate to Hell](#embed:https://youtu.be/o-aseqW0iiU?t=8380)

Nerissa Juliet would also add her own deviousness to the plot:

[A spy for the Church.](#embed:https://youtu.be/o-aseqW0iiU?t=13239)

Nerissa Juliet had accidentally won Roa of the Illumination's trust, been brought into the fold, and would be told the truth when the morrow comes. She would be able to bring Roa closer to the 'plot' and drag her further into her madness.

But conspiracies against those who stood in their way weren't the only thing that bound the two.

For in the darkest of nights, you can find the most shining of stars; a genuine friendship that can last through worlds and time for these two Sisters of the Cloth.
