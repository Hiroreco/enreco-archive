It was almost funny, really. With her memories of her past self gone, it felt like she'd become nothing more than an empty container for everyone else's shenanigans.

Maybe today would finally be peaceful. Just for once. She was only a supplier, after all. People kept throwing fancy titles at her—Queen Tam Gandr this, Queen Tam Gandr that—but that wasn't who she was. She was just Tam. A humble supplier. And suppliers absolutely did not need this much chaos and attention. She'd repeated that fact so many times she'd long since lost count.

[Oh boy, here we go](#embed:https://www.youtube.com/live/JZ2gO4qDJ2w?si=LCJ7Dy9bebQjnA_Q&t=516)

Knowing how quick-tempered and impatient she could get, Bae, Roa, and Gonathon accompanied the blue maiden into the Labyrinth of Emotion. Unfortunately for everyone involved, Tam's temper flared almost immediately after they failed to solve a [puzzle](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JZ2gO4qDJ2w&t=1872).

Afterward, she returned to the kingdom to prepare for the next dungeon conquest, though along the way people kept bothering her about the ["pet"](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JZ2gO4qDJ2w&t=4113) sitting on her head. It was just her pet, wasn't it? Everyone had been weirdly obsessed with it for days now.

Either way, she needed to hurry. If she could just finish everything quickly and call it a day before getting dragged into more nonsense, maybe she'd survive with her sanity intact.

Sadly for poor Tam, it wasn't just her peers determined to test her patience.

[You thief! You're disgusting!](#embed:https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JZ2gO4qDJ2w&t=4319)

She *just* wanted to enter the new labyrinth, but somehow the journey there turned into a ridiculous wild goose chase filled with long, unnecessary, [tedious chores.](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JZ2gO4qDJ2w&t=4936) Needless to say, she was not having a good day.

[She's very calm, you see](#embed:https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JZ2gO4qDJ2w&t=5375)

Once the prerequisites were finally fulfilled, she reached the [Labyrinth of Song](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JZ2gO4qDJ2w&t=7755), where she recovered the memories of Nerissa Ravencroft. After a long, exhausting day filled with chores, yelling, and barely-contained rage, Tam had finally caught up with everyone else.

She could've ended the day there.

But you know what? Why not explore a little? Surely she'd suffered enough already. Surely fate wouldn't dare throw even *more* problems at her now, right?

...Right?

Around town, people were building strange structures here and there, while odd [sheep-like](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JZ2gO4qDJ2w&t=9325) monuments had begun appearing randomly throughout the kingdom. Apparently they were evil, and Roa had requested that everyone destroy them on sight. Suspicious, certainly, but honestly not even close to the weirdest thing Tam had seen lately.

Then there was the church everyone kept talking about.

Tam had heard that the previous day, a "funeral" had taken place there, where Shiori displayed Shakira's body in front of everyone as a memorial. Sure enough, a coffin really was hanging from the ceiling.

...Wait.

What was that underneath it?

At the center podium of the quartz chapel, Tam noticed a deep hole descending into darkness below. And, like every curious human before her, she made the worst possible decision.

She looked down, braced herself, and jumped.

[W-wuh what just happened...?](#embed:https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JZ2gO4qDJ2w&t=9452)

The moment she landed, she immediately realized she'd stumbled onto something she absolutely was not supposed to see.

Deep underground, Shiori and Nerissa were working on some kind of secret project, and the second they noticed Tam standing there, they immediately ordered her to leave. Or rather, they *forced* her to leave. The two erased her memories of the encounter entirely before sending her back to the surface.

...

Alrighty then.

Unfortunately, erasing someone's memories and resetting them to the exact same mental state as before tends to produce predictable results, as Tam wandered back to the same chapel later on. This time, however, there was no mysterious hole to jump into.

Surely nothing bad would happen after this, and the day would finally end peacefully.

...But of course, things could never be that simple.

Suddenly, Tam found herself accused of peeping on Shiori while she was changing. Then more people piled onto the accusations. Everyone was yelling, people were crying, others were saying increasingly bizarre things, and somewhere in the middle of it all people started demanding that she remove her hat—

AHHHHHHH!

She really should've just gone home after the dungeons.

[Bro what did I doooo](#embed:https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JZ2gO4qDJ2w&t=10597)

It was a lot to handle. But still, amidst all the chaos, there was at least one small silver lining.

Tam wasn't alone.

Someone else had gone through the same insanity alongside her, just as confused and overwhelmed as she was. The one person she felt she could trust most throughout the ordeal. The same person who, no matter how many times she corrected him, continued insisting on calling her "Queen."

(See [Gonathon-Tam](#edge:gigi-kronii))

And so, after everything was finally over, Tam settled down inside the library, documenting the chaos-filled, utterly [wild day.](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JZ2gO4qDJ2w&t=13231)
