**IN MY FEELINGS**

*Chilled straight down to the bone*

There was nothing more horrifying than waking up and finding yourself in a horror movie. Yet, Nerissa Juliet seemed unbothered by what was set before her eyes. [Strange advertisements?](https://youtu.be/60_Pduc0ODY?t=425) were posted on the facade of their home, trails of blood spilled on the grass, as if someone just committed a mass murder. 

She explored the vicinity and realized this part of the town was infested by the ominous poster that read, "The Illumination."

Even the floor inside the house was bloodstained, with cryptic letters stored in their chest.

Curious, she delved into the mystery further. As she visited each home, it became more apparent that all of them may have suffered the same [DOOM](https://youtu.be/60_Pduc0ODY?t=979):

> Blood spilled  
> leading to the giant sheep  
> letters filled  
> with words so deep.

*Haunted by the ghost of you*

As if that wasn't enough chaos. Another terror was brought upon the Kingdom of Modern Libestal in the form of a ghost. 

Nerissa Juliet first encountered the phantom of [Shakira's body](https://youtu.be/60_Pduc0ODY?t=3140) floating before her in the Guild Hall. As always, she remained unperturbed. *[Slay,  Queen!](https://youtu.be/60_Pduc0ODY?t=3357)*

**SHATTER**

[*It's me, myself, and I*](embed:https://youtu.be/60_Pduc0ODY?t=4775)

Nerissa Juliet, along with her party, Sister Shiori, the twins Lil Witch Mococo and Fuwawa, and Lady Bloodflame, now broke into the Labyrinth of Song.

Did it ring a bell? Indeed, for Nerissa, something about this dungeon felt oddly familiar. 

The party was teleported to a platform floating in the air. Around were endless loops of several black bridges that seemed to be forming a musical staff. Below was the beautiful palace waiting to be explored. 

Like notes gliding on a music sheet, the group crossed the bridge, cautious of falling. Yet, one by one, they fell and had to do it over again.

Even Nerissa wasn't spared. Bird as she was, her wings proved to be too short to even [let her fly](https://youtu.be/60_Pduc0ODY?t=4939). 

[*The memories you can't fight*](embed:https://youtu.be/60_Pduc0ODY?t=6651)

As soon as they conquered the dungeon, a flood of memories overwhelmed our princess. The vision she saw revealed that she was indeed not human or a witch, but a demon. A demon with a voice capable of... saving the lives of others? 

That bit unsettled her.  As it was rather unfamiliar and conflicting with the ones she could now recall. 

Was she truly a hero? Wasn't she someone supposed to be feared?

**SAY MY NAME**

*If you're down to get familiarized*

Despite her memories returning, Nerissa "Juliet" Ravencroft remained uncertain about her own [identity](https://youtu.be/60_Pduc0ODY?t=7097). What she saw in the vision didn't make any sense. At all. 

What about her memories of being a prisoner? Locked in a cellar along with the other members of the Coven. Didn't her voice used to torment people? 

These fragments... only left her with [more questions](https://youtu.be/60_Pduc0ODY?t=7243) than answers.

[*Fallen fast under my spell (no more?)*](https://youtu.be/60_Pduc0ODY?t=7940)

As if that was not too much to bear already, another dilemma struck her when she spoke with Prisoner Zero. 

What makes humans (or, in Nerissa's case, a demon) free? The prisoner dared her to prove it by giving up the most precious thing to her.

Nerissa knew what that was. Or rather, WHO it was. 

Contemplating, she wondered if she could ever truly let go of her "Romeo." Just thinking about it broke her, but what if it was the true path to her freedom?

She had to. Right? Even if she was terrified of the outcome. Even if it meant Lady Bloodflame would no longer look her way again.

But wait... did she *really* have to [break their bond?](https://youtu.be/60_Pduc0ODY?t=8222) (See [Liz-Nerissa](#edge:liz-nerissa))
