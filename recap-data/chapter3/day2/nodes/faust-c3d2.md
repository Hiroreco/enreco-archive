After learning about the existence of "Nullspace" (see [Fia](#node:fia)), the heroes sought out Faust in hopes of uncovering more information about the mysterious figure said to know the way there.

[The "all-seeing madman"](#embed:https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Up0bZJINZx8&t=6698)

The so-called "all-seeing madman," also known as the prisoner of Cell 0414, was described as someone capable of perceiving things others could not: hidden doorways, secret messages, even the private thoughts of those around them. If anyone knew how to reach Nullspace, it would likely be them.

Unfortunately, approaching the prisoner was far from simple.

The madness surrounding Cell 0414 had long since seeped into the hallway itself. Anyone who attempted to walk toward the cell would gradually become disoriented as the world around them distorted and twisted. Paths would loop endlessly, directions would lose meaning, and before long, those attempting the journey would unknowingly wander right back to the entrance where they had started.

However, Faust revealed there _might_ be a way through the madness.

A special artifact known as a "glass compass" could supposedly guide travelers through the distorted hallway without allowing them to lose their way. With it, reaching the all-seeing madman might finally become possible.

And so, the heroes' next objective became clear: locate a mysterious glass compass, then investigate the prisoner of Cell 0414 themselves.
