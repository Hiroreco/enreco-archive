They were two of the most prestigious knights the kingdom had to offer, and they had fought side by side in countless battles before.

Though their relationship had started somewhat rocky, time slowly forged mutual respect between them, and eventually, they became friends. Good ones, even.

But no bridge of comradeship, no matter how sturdy, can remain whole forever once cracks begin to form. Given enough reason, those fractures only grow until eventually, the bridge collapses under its own weight.

[A decision, and the price paid for it](#embed:https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2rnclFeQ5EM&t=4700)

Elizabeth chose not to trust Fia.

Gonathon, however, did.

Despite her best attempts to explain that she had done it because of an oath she swore, Gonathon questioned the very friendship they had built together. And despite Elizabeth’s own desperate attempts to preserve that bond, Gonathon still held doubts. He still felt betrayed nonetheless.

Elizabeth had changed, but was that change truly the right one?

She now wielded her blade for those near and dear to her. Yet Gonathon, in that moment, stood like a reflection of her former self, someone who swore unwavering loyalty to what was right above all else.

What if he was the one in the right?

Worse still, what if they were to meet again on the battlefield?

Once comrades who fought together, they might now be forced to turn their blades against one another.

And if that day ever came, the scarlet maiden urged Gonathon not to hold back. She asked him to remain the kind and just knight he had always been, and Gonathon agreed.

The decision of which side to stand on had been made in mere moments, yet the fracture it caused would linger far longer than either of them wished.
