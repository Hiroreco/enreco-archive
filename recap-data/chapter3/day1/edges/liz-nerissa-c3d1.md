Within the traveling library that moved through time, Lady Bloodflame found herself whisked away by The Raven One.

"Let's run for it," Nerissa Juliet said, as if there were some danger they needed to escape from.

Her Juliet took her outside to watch the stars—the beauty that surrounded them within the void. In this time of uncertainty, it felt like something precious to look up to.

Lady Bloodflame looked at her, her eyes sparkling with awe and wonder. _"What a truly beautiful insight indeed."_

But when she lowered her gaze and met Nerissa's eyes, her true purpose was revealed. Nerissa confessed her doubts about Fia. About their quests as heroes. [Can she really be trusted?](#embed:https://www.youtube.com/live/2rnclFeQ5EM?si=modXYx2D4fvWS49x&t=1555) Lady Bloodflame was unsure. She was torn. Where should her loyalty lie?

But she was a knight, and she had sworn an oath to her princess, her Juliet. And so, it was an oath she must honor.

To remain by her side and protect her.

["Maybe...she is my Libestal?"](#embed:https://www.youtube.com/live/2rnclFeQ5EM?si=NjEE5HnmVAzopTwN&t=2432)

Some doubts could not be purged as quickly as one wished. Somehow, Lady Bloodflame still found herself in a bind. What if there was truth to Fia's claims after all? They _had_ saved the world, albeit at a cost. And yet, she could not break her promise to Nerissa either. She had hurt her once before. She would—and _should_—never make the same mistake again.

As if making a decision blindly, she chose to side with Nix and the Rebels in honor of her pledge of loyalty to Nerissa and her sister, Mococo.

When the time came for her to decide what occupation to take, she did not think about what she personally wanted to do. Instead, she called Nerissa and asked what would be most helpful to her. Juliet took advantage of this and told her she needed a supplier. Without hesitation, Lady Bloodflame gave in to her wish and became one, abandoning her own self for the sake of love.
