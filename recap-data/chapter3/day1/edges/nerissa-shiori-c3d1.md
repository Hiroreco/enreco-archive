The fragments of the past remained fresh in her mind. As if it were all just yesterday.

She remembered her pact with the Coven, where she and Sister Shiori belonged. The Archiver did not deny it, but she was reluctant to admit it was "evil".

Nerissa Juliet's truth seemed to be rather distorted from what Shiori recalled. After all, the Rebellion was never intended to become something sinister!

Was it?

Regardless of whether their group was indeed evil or not, Nerissa Juliet regarded Sister Shiori as an ally.

[Platonic Beaches](#embed:https://youtu.be/To_P9pUvMRA?t=3857)

As they explored and delved deeper into this new realm, the two women eventually took on the occupation of chefs. A profession similar to the one they held in the past.

Perhaps it was comfort. Or maybe familiarity.

Or perhaps they were both simply clinging to fragments of identities long lost to time.

Nerissa Juliet, at the very least, was certainly holding onto hers.

The two chef journeyed across the kingdom in search of the freshest quality ingredients they could find to complete their cake-making quest. The Raven Maiden offered to help her comrade. Unfortunately, as they took on different paths, they lost each other along the way.

After spending some time searching, Nerissa informed Shiori of a promising location for gathering fresh eggs. To her surprise, Shiori, too, discovered one. However, eventually revealed that she had to kill just to get it. An act that was undoubtedly evil.

The Maven rebuked her, but the nun defended herself by insisting that she had no choice as a struggling peasant. What began as a moral criticism suddenly turned into playful banter. Then, somehow, flirting.

Shiori offered Nerissa to become the first of her "beaches". To which, our fair maiden politely declined, admitting she had already devoted herself to another. (Gee, I wonder who that could be...)

An Anomaly that smelled like home

This time, Sister Shiori called Nerissa Juliet on the phone, informing her of a mysterious place she had found. She later revealed that it felt familiar somehow, reminding her of their old [home](https://youtu.be/To_P9pUvMRA?t=4901) from the distant past. A world unknown to the Ancient nor to the Modern Libestal.

The revelation piqued her interest. And so, Nerissa listened attentively, in full anticipation as Shiori spoke more. But, as the Archiver delved deeper, the call was cut short.

What did Shiori mean by"It smelled like home"? Why did the call end abruptly? What secrets would await her, and was this mystery truly connected to the identities they once possessed prior to each version of Libestal?

Nerissa Juliet had yet to find out.
