Fia sat within her library, the place she had called home for what felt like an eternity by now.

After all, there was nowhere else left for her to go.

She had been forced to watch her kingdom fall into ruin, tearfully abandoning it as destruction consumed everything she once knew. She had tried to defy that fate, tried to prevent it, tried to save him—the Outlander—but in the end, it had all led to the same conclusion regardless.

And yet, perhaps there was still a silver lining.

[The next step](#embed:https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2rnclFeQ5EM&t=1047)

She had once attempted to “cheat” fate by breaking away from the past, only for everything to arrive at the same doomed ending. So maybe the answer was the opposite.

Maybe, if she followed the path that was meant to happen instead of resisting it, things might finally change.

It was a desperate thought. A fragile one.

Even more so now that some, perhaps many, of her heroes—her Shining Stars—no longer trusted her.

Nonetheless, she had to try. Trying was the only thing she had left.

Fia spoke of the “Lost Worlds,” worlds that had somehow survived the destruction they were fated to suffer. And through the heroes’ memories of the Libestal they once journeyed through, she managed to guide the Library into the present-day Libestal, which somehow still stood, having survived the Ruin.

To keep contact with the heroes, she gave each of them a FiaPhone, as she herself could not directly manifest within this world.

Those phones were soon taken by a mysterious local rebel named Nix, who altered them so that she could communicate with the heroes as well.

And then came the choice.

[Rebellion vs Shinging Stars](#embed:https://www.youtube.com/live/2rnclFeQ5EM?si=gc6xDzQakf3gCmrw&t=2787)

Nix asked the heroes to decide whether they would join the “Rebellion” or remain with the “Shining Stars.” Just as Fia herself had once said, once they arrived in this world, the heroes alone would decide what path to take next.

And so, the first dilemma revealed itself:

Would they stand beside Fia?

Or would they turn their backs on her entirely?
