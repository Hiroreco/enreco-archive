The journey thus far had been a strange one, to say the least.

First, they had been brought to a kingdom under the pretense of saving it, only to discover it was all a fabrication created by an evil being that deceived them into setting it free. Then came the desperate attempt to travel into the past to stop that very being from ever coming into existence. They failed. And now, they found themselves once again within a library drifting through time and space, surrounded by others trapped in the same predicament… alongside a talking cat plushie.

It sounded like fiction. A bizarre one, certainly.

Yet through all of these rapid, overwhelming changes, one thing remained steadfast within the scarlet maiden: the duty of a knight.

To care for others.

To protect others.

Because to Elizabeth, they meant everything.

Despite her unease regarding the current situation, Elizabeth immediately checked on her comrades to ensure they were safe. [Mococo,](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2rnclFeQ5EM&t=1779) her beloved sister, was the first to greet her. Lady Bloodflame, overjoyed to see her, showered her with affectionate headpats.

The next person she sought out was someone even closer to her heart.

The one who guided her when she was lost. The one who stayed by her side during her lowest moments. The one she considered her dearest friend, [Shakira.](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2rnclFeQ5EM&t=1401)

The two spoke briefly, and because Lady Bloodflame trusted her so deeply, she confided her lingering uneasiness about leaving behind the world they once knew. More importantly, she questioned whether Fia could truly be trusted at all.

But their conversation was interrupted by the arrival of the one person capable of shaking Lady Bloodflame’s world more than anyone else.

[It's turning into a strawberry again](#embed:https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2rnclFeQ5EM&t=1401s)

Everything they had experienced together thus far—the pain, the memories, the feelings tangled between them—was complicated. The scarlet knight had many things she wished to ask Nerissa, many things she wished to say. But more pressing matters stood before them, so she buried those thoughts away for another time (see [Liz-Nerissa](#edge:liz-nerissa)).

Still, one important thing did emerge from her conversations with Nerissa and the others. None of them felt they [could trust](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2rnclFeQ5EM&t=1865) Fia. Not fully.

Perhaps it was instinct. Perhaps it was fear. Perhaps it was because Fia once stood beside the Outlander before he became the Outsider. Whatever the reason, her words carried too much uncertainty to accept blindly. As Fia’s knight, Lady Bloodflame remained by her Princess’ side.

Yet even she carried doubts.

Eventually, the time came for the heroes to return to Libestal. A thousand years had passed, and the kingdom was no longer the same place they once knew.

Upon arriving, Lady Bloodflame was immediately confronted with a choice.

Nix, one of the citizens of modern Libestal, stood against the kingdom’s system and sought to incite rebellion. After helping Elizabeth avoid an incident with the guards, Nix presented her with an ultimatum: remain loyal to Fia, or join the Rebellion.

And in perhaps the most unexpected twist of all… Elizabeth chose the latter.

[I must keep my promise](#embed:https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2rnclFeQ5EM&t=2872)

Lady Bloodflame chose not to place her faith in Fia. Instead, she chose the Rebellion. She chose to become Nerissa’s knight. She chose to protect Mococo.

Elizabeth had changed tremendously since the beginning of this enigmatic journey. Once upon a time, she was the ideal knight—someone who upheld duty above all else, who existed solely to serve her ruler.

But that version of her had already begun to disappear long ago, back when she cast aside the blade meant for her king and instead wielded it to protect the people she loved.

And now, that change had become clearer than ever before. She was willing to defy Fia herself for their sake.

[A blade for others](#embed:https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2rnclFeQ5EM&t=3587s)

Gonathan, however, did not share her conviction. A fellow knight who valued honor and justice as deeply as she once did, he was devastated by her decision (see [Gonathon-Elizabeth](#edge:gigi-liz)).

...

This [time,](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2rnclFeQ5EM&t=3688) Elizabeth chose to become a Supplier. And just like the journeys before this one, she quickly found herself confused—not only by the mechanics of the job, but by the world itself. Still a dunce, it seemed.

She carried out her quests and duties as usual, and for a while, everything seemed relatively normal.

Then disaster struck.

[Ughahg the friggg?!](#embed:https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2rnclFeQ5EM&t=6907)

Nerissa called for Lady Bloodflame to deliver devastating news.

Shakira had passed away.

The knight rushed toward the others, desperately hoping it was some sort of misunderstanding. But it was true. Her partner, her closest confidant, her best friend… was gone. As she struggled to process the loss, Elizabeth suddenly thought of Roa, who was likely suffering more than any of them. She sought her out and found her utterly inconsolable.

Roa had lost the love of her life.

Their marriage had been short, but the love they shared was no less real because of it. And even though Roa had once pursued Lady Bloodflame’s heart herself, Elizabeth still chose to comfort her during her darkest moment, reassuring her that she was not alone.

Perhaps those words helped.

[This is... so sad... _eats carrot_](#embed:https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2rnclFeQ5EM&t=7273s)

And somehow, despite everything, Lady Bloodflame continued onward rather quickly for someone who had just lost a person so dear to her.

But perhaps that was simply what knights did.

Or perhaps it was all she knew how to do.

As the day continued, tasks were completed, old friendships reconciled, a new home established, and difficult choices made for the sake of her Princess and her sister.

But what awaited Lady Bloodflame next?

Were the choices she made truly the correct ones?

Or would they lead her toward something far darker than anything she had experienced before?

Only time would tell...
