Their final day in Libestal and our Peasant's feelings still lingered sorely for Immerkind.

Not even the chuuni Nix could draw Peasant's attention as strongly as another.

Throughout her day, Bae's eyes lingered on the green-haired knight as her heart fluttered with distress whenever she spoke to her.

To love again and not say a word, or breathe her confession and hope for the best. In a perfect, kinder world, the answer would be obvious. A bright answer that promised a future.

Unfortunately, it wasn't the Peasant's world; the reality she lived was one where one fought monsters to make a living, and those you trust may be the ones to stab you in the back.

But, Peasant The Bae was no longer the cowardly rat that ran away screaming when she first woke in these strange lands. No, the Peasant had found her courage and gold, and had adopted her kids (who are currently, definitely on an adventure somewhere else) and Polar the Bear.

Perhaps this time, Peasant The Bae can find another to call her own. At first, it went well, [blushing with flowers in hand](https://youtu.be/Qh33RM1yOtI?t=1560), Peasant gave it to Immerkind. It was sweet, filled with thoughts of kindness and an innocent love.

A gift that Immerkind graciously accepted, thanking the Peasant for thinking of her fondly. It encouraged Bae; perhaps her intentions were getting through. Perhaps... there was an inkling of love there for her after all.

The ring in her pocket felt heavier at the prospect.

[Bae confessed a second time.](#embed:https://youtu.be/Qh33RM1yOtI?t=7203)

Again, she bore her heart for Immerkind to see. Straight (well, gay) and direct, refusing to allow any misunderstandings to occur. If Peasant didn't survive the Dungeon, she refused to die in regret due to an unspoken confession.

The knight's response had to wait. There was one last mission to complete. One last boss battle to fight before our hero can meet her knight.

It was fierce, but Peasant refused to let any harm come to Immerkind. She may be but a peasant, but she had gotten stronger. From the trials she had faced, to the truths she witnessed, too many dice had rolled for Bae to remain on square 1.

Everything had aligned, and Peasant and the rest faced the Outsider again. But, as they were ambushed by the Curse again, Fia was able to transport them back to safety.

There, Peasant faced Immerkind, ever unready to hear her answer, but refused to let things remain unsaid.

"Peasant," there was something soft to Immerkind as she gave a [bouquet of roses](https://youtu.be/euqA3GB8Jc0?t=13129) to Bae.

For a moment, Peasant was stricken, her mind flashing back to another time. Was Immerkind returning the flowers Peasant had given her earlier? But no, it turns out these were flowers Immerkind herself had bought for her.

Immerkind brought up the Peasant's last confession, from a week ago, even if it had felt like a year and a month, and asked, why again?

She reached deep in her heart, the distance she gave, the dreams she had. It had all revolved around Immerkind.

Was Immerkind's answer still the same?

"Yes."

Unloved. Unreciprocated.

Unfortunately, there was no lake or river for Bae to drown in.

Again, just like a week ago, Bae swore to only love her gold.

Gold was the only thing that mattered in the end.
