> You meddle with my cold, cold lips,
> My breath smells earthy strong;
> If you have one kiss of my clay-cold lips,
> Your time will not be long.

> 'Tis down in yonder groove, sweetheart,
> Where you and I walked o'er;
> The finest flower that e'er was seen
> Is withered to the stalk.

> The stalk is withered dry, my love,
> So must our hearts decay;
> So make yourself content, my love,
> Till God calls you away.

\- _"The Unquiet Grave"_
