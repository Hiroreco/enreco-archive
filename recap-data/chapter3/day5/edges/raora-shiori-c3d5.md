The antagonistic encounters with Shiori and Roa continue.

Shiori claimed that Roa and she have had the same goal all this time. Both of them want the same thing. At least Shiori thinks so.

Rejecting the idea, Roa points out that working with the gods isn't part of her plan.

But the sister says:

[Them is who?](#embed:https://www.youtube.com/live/rFmdOarYXuE?t=862)

Their hostility was replaced by a chilling consensus; for a moment, they would stop shouting and start listening. They forged a promise to meet again under the looming shadow of the church, a location that felt less like a meeting place and more like an inevitable grave at this point.

Later during the day, as Roa showed the castle Gap to Nerissa and Bijou, the nun appears.
Like the nimble panther that she is, Roa rushed in to break the scaffolding that Shiori was standing on. But then, Shiori replied with a hollow, mocking pity:

[Oh. What a shame.](#embed:https://www.youtube.com/live/rFmdOarYXuE?t=11619)

As the night arrived, the Sister of the Cloth approached the gathered members of the Illumination. Standing her ground, Roa demanded Shiori to retreat, confidently declaring that the Sister did not belong in a space reserved for the enlightened.

Shiori responded with a sly, mocking giggle, questioning if Roa was truly certain of her standing.

In a shocking shift of loyalty, both Nerissa and Bijou moved away from Roa’s side, positioning themselves like silent sentinels behind Shiori. Roa’s most trusted allies remained mute, their gazes fixed uncaringly upon their former leader.

Seizing the moment, Shiori [ridiculed](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rFmdOarYXuE&t=11660) Roa for her sudden isolation, dismissing the Illumination as a pathetic joke and suggesting that Roa was better off alone. And yet, Shiori claimed there was a "better way" to transcend their reality. She proposed that Roa’s own latent power was the hidden key to unlocking the door to salvation and saving everyone from their confinement. Shiori extended a hand in a gesture of false grace, but Roa, overwhelmed by the suffocating atmosphere of betrayal, fled into the night.

Racing through the night, Nerissa [caught](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rFmdOarYXuE&t=12035) up with Roa, urging her to at least entertain the Sister's proposal. Though Roa branded her a traitor, the witch refuted the accusation.

She argued that entering the church was a strategic necessity; to prove Shiori wrong, they first had to understand the nature of her plan. Nerissa bound herself to a promise that she and Bijou would remain by Roa’s side, ensuring that when the time for the final confrontation arrived, it would be three against one.

Upon reaching the [church](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rFmdOarYXuE&t=12220), they noticed the Sister of the Cloth standing high above them on the rooftop. The silver light of the moon illuminated her form, stretching an oppressive shadow across the ground on which Roa was standing.

Roa entered the sanctuary, but her hollow safety shattered when Shakira’s rotting corpse arrived. Shiori and Nerissa watched with chilling indifference as Roa was lured into an abyssal hole beneath the church.

They emerged in a subterranean vault of architectural abominations; obsidian ceilings throbbed with lesion-like nerves while viridian floors led toward taunting golden doors. Amidst a miasmic slurry of sulfur, Roa’s mind fractured. Hallucinating beauty over the cadaver's rot, she was escorted to a jaundiced altar.

Forced to read [blasphemous writings](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rFmdOarYXuE&t=12690), a spark of survival ignited; Roa fled toward the surface. Yet, Nerissa’s obsidian staff struck with predatory grace, slugging Roa into concussive darkness. Dragged back by her hair, Roa thrashed with animalistic urgency, but Nerissa’s iron grip pinned her to the ritual pages. An arcane spell pried her jaw open, forcing her to vomit out the ink-borne malice of the text.

As Roa slumped, a hollowed-out husk, Bijou emerged to confess her role as Shakira’s [murderer](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rFmdOarYXuE&t=12830). This final betrayal obliterated Roa’s remaining sanity. Amidst sobbing agony, the coven cast her twitching frame into a pulsating crimson stone. The constricting geometry crushed the remaining life from her lungs, ending her suffering in a permanent, stygian silence.
