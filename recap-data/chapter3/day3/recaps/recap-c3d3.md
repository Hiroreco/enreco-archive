## Day 3: A Plagued Kingdom and a Forgotten Memorial

The second dungeon marked yet another step closer to finding the Outsider: the **Labyrinth of Emotions.** Once again, our heroes were forced to confront unfamiliar trials, uncover hidden truths, and reclaim fragments of memories long lost to time. This time, the memories belonged to **Koseki Bijou.**

Yet beyond the revelations hidden within the dungeon lay a far more peculiar discovery, one that felt less like an answer and more like a warning. A glimpse into something far greater waiting beyond the horizon. Perhaps a hint, perhaps merely a taste of what was still to come.

Something big was coming.

> A dark, malevolent aura that hangs like a curse...
> over the five who started the Rebellion.

![Battle against the Prisoner of Emotions](/images-opt/labyrinth-of-emotions-opt.webp)

And much like this "malevolent aura," another strange atmosphere had begun spreading throughout the kingdom, an ailing one. The contrast was impossible to ignore. While some continued their daily routines as if nothing had changed, others were visibly deteriorating. Some suffered from illnesses with no clear cause, while others were consumed by their own spiraling thoughts and beliefs, becoming shadows of themselves.

Lady Bloodflame had grown paler than ever before. At first it had been subtle, easy to dismiss, but now everyone could see that something was terribly wrong. Then there was Bijou herself, who was not merely unwell, but _sick_ sick—though hers was at least a genuine illness, one far easier to cure than the invisible afflictions consuming the former.

And finally, there was Roa, whose mental state continued to worsen with every passing moment. Ever since uncovering truths she was never meant to know, ever since seeing that sheep—no, _the_ sheep—her fear and paranoia toward this world had become more terrifying than ever. Even the mere sight of such creatures was enough to send her spiraling into panic and despair.

Whatever plagued the kingdom, it was clear that many were no longer in their best state, physically or mentally. Doubts began to form between some, while trust was steadily lost between others. Something invisible was poisoning the air around them all.

And all of it eventually led to what was _supposed_ to be the grand highlight of the day: a funeral for the one who had only managed to stay with everyone for less than half a day. Organized by a devoted Sister who worked tirelessly day and night preparing every detail, wishing only to preserve the memory of the departed... and perhaps earn a little profit along the way. Yet when the moment finally arrived, it passed like a gust of wind through an empty chapel. The candles burned, prayers were spoken, tears threatened to fall, but by the time the final bell rang, the world had already moved on.

A memorial built to remember someone, only to become forgotten itself.
