Roa questioned Sister Shiori’s loyalty, wondering if she had already fallen to _the sheep_. She called Shiori blind to the truth, but the Sister of the Flock calmly refuted the accusation, insisting that her sight reached far deeper than anyone else within the city.

The monochrome maiden then revealed something far more unsettling: she knew about the Gap, and about the border Roa had crossed beyond reality itself. Shiori openly agreed that this world was nothing more than a fabrication, a false reality forced upon them all. A story they had no choice but to inhabit.

Yet unlike Roa, Shiori claimed to have accepted it.

She described herself as a prisoner of the narrative, someone fully aware of the truth yet forever trapped within the confines of the script.

[I know what you know.](#embed:https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MGo9h3BNyj0&t=12570)

What began as a tense discussion quickly escalated into something far worse: a declaration of war.

Roa rejected every justification Shiori offered, condemning her as nothing more than a slave to the gods and a servant of the Flock. In return, Shiori questioned Roa’s methods and obsession with uncovering the so-called mastermind behind everything.

But to Roa, there was no mastermind left to find.

There was only the system itself.

And Shiori was serving it willingly.

With paranoia, grief, and fury intertwining into one overwhelming emotion, Roa issued a final chilling warning for the Sister to stay away from her.

From that moment onward, Roa officially declared Sister Shiori... her enemy.
